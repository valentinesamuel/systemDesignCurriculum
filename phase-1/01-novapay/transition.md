# Leaving NovaPay

Eight months. That's how long you spend at NovaPay before the company pivots.
Not a death — more of a strategic contraction. Kwik's integration went live,
flash sale traffic peaked at 11,200 RPM, and the system held. Priya called it
"the best stress test we didn't plan." The PCI-DSS assessment passed with two
minor findings, both remediated within the quarter.

But then the Series C fell through. Headcount reduction. Non-essential teams first.
The payments core team — four engineers building critical infrastructure — was
deemed "above headcount ratio for stage." You see the writing on the wall two weeks
before the announcement. You update your resume.

Your last week, Dani buys the team lunch. "You shipped good work," she says,
which from Dani is the equivalent of a standing ovation.

---

**Slack DM — Marcus Webb → You (your last day)**

**Marcus Webb**
Good run. You learned the right things for the wrong reasons — which is how
most engineers learn. PCI the hard way is better than PCI not at all.
One piece of advice for wherever you go next: the systems are different.
The problems are the same.

---

**Three weeks later**

You get a recruiter message for **MeridianHealth**, a Series B health-tech company
building an EHR (Electronic Health Records) integration platform for mid-sized
hospital networks. The stack is familiar: Node.js, Postgres, Redis. The domain
is completely foreign: healthcare, HIPAA, patient data.

The recruiter says the role is for a "Senior Backend Engineer — Platform."
You ask about the team. She mentions the current tech lead is leaving.
You ask why. She pauses. "It's a great opportunity," she says.

You take the interview anyway.

---

**Day 1 at MeridianHealth**

The engineering manager, **Ravi Chandran**, shakes your hand and immediately
apologizes. "I should warn you," he says. "We've had some... architectural
decisions that we're not proud of. You're going to find things."

He's not wrong.
