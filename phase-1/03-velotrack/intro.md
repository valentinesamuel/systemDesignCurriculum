# VeloTrack — Company Introduction

VeloTrack is a Y Combinator-backed logistics startup building the real-time delivery
tracking infrastructure for last-mile delivery networks across Southeast Asia and
Australia. Think of them as the backend engine powering delivery apps — they ingest
location pings from hundreds of thousands of drivers, process delivery state machines,
and serve real-time ETAs to consumers and merchant dashboards.

The company is nine engineers moving very fast, processing 4 million delivery events
per day with aspirations (and a VC mandate) to hit 20 million within the quarter.
The stack is Node.js, Kafka, PostgreSQL, and Redis — with a Kafka setup that has
organically grown into something that works until it doesn't.

You join as a **Senior Backend Engineer** on the Platform team — effectively the
second-most experienced engineer after the CTO, **Emeka Eze**. Emeka is brilliant,
builds fast, and has a healthy respect for systems he didn't design himself. The
previous platform hire lasted six weeks before burning out. You ask why. Emeka says:
"The pager. It pages a lot."

Your first day starts with a Kafka post-mortem document that Emeka has left open
on the conference room screen.
