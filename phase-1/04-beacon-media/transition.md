# Leaving Beacon Media

A year and a half. It goes fast when the problems are interesting.

The CDN hit rate is at 91.3% — not quite 92%, but Fatima has stopped mentioning
the 2% gap. Monthly CDN cost dropped from $2.05M to $1.1M. The notification system
handled 9.2 million pushes for the Champions League final without missing a beat.
The presigned URL system has been running for 14 months with zero piracy incidents
reported by ContentArmor.

The MediaVault transcoding pipeline — 20,000 videos in 68 hours, 4 hours under
deadline. The penalty was never triggered. Fatima's quarterly all-hands thank-you
slide had your name on it with the subtitle "saved us $360,000 in penalties."

You leave because an opportunity comes up that you can't say no to. Not for the
money — the money is fine at Beacon. For the problem.

**AgroSense** is building agricultural monitoring infrastructure using IoT sensors
deployed across farms in East Africa, India, and Southeast Asia. 800,000 sensors
sending soil moisture, temperature, and GPS data every 30 seconds. Time-series
data at scale, edge computing, and analytics pipelines.

You've done payments, healthcare, logistics, and media. You've never built
anything with physical hardware as the data source.

The Head of Engineering, **Priya Ranganathan**, says: "We're drowning in sensor data
we can't process. The farmers who depend on this data are making decisions on
information that's 6 hours old. That's crops dying. Literally."

That lands differently than most pitches.
