# AgroSense — Company Introduction

AgroSense builds agricultural intelligence infrastructure for smallholder and
commercial farms across East Africa, India, and Southeast Asia. Their IoT platform
ingests data from 800,000 field sensors: soil moisture probes, temperature/humidity
stations, irrigation controllers, and GPS-tracked crop monitoring units. That data
is processed, analyzed, and delivered back to farmers and agronomists through a
mobile app and web dashboard.

The mission is real: farmers in Kenya use AgroSense data to decide when to irrigate
and when to harvest. Decisions made on stale or missing data can cost a smallholder
farmer their entire season's income. The stakes are not abstract.

The engineering team is 25 people. The Head of Engineering, **Priya Ranganathan**,
came from AWS IoT and has strong opinions about time-series data design. The
platform is technically sound at 200,000 sensors. At 800,000 — and growing — the
seams are showing.

You join as a **Senior Backend Engineer** on the Data Platform team. Your first
briefing is a 30-minute war room session with Priya and the data team, who are
staring at a Grafana panel showing a 6-hour data processing lag.

"We know the data is there," Priya says. "We just can't get to it fast enough."
