# PulseCommerce — Company Introduction

PulseCommerce is a Series C B2B e-commerce platform built for mid-market retailers —
think Shopify for companies with 100,000 to 10 million SKUs and enterprise-grade
operational requirements. Their platform powers 8,000 merchants, hosts 150 million
product listings, and processes $2.1 billion in GMV annually.

The product has grown much faster than the underlying infrastructure. The search
experience is the most visible symptom: a buyer searching for "waterproof running
shoes size 10 black" often returns results that are technically correct but
practically useless — out-of-stock items, discontinued products, or items from
the wrong merchant on a multi-merchant storefront.

The engineering team is 60 people. **Nnamdi Okafor**, Head of Engineering, is
measured and strategic — he's been at PulseCommerce since the B series and has
watched the infrastructure debt accumulate. He gives you a comprehensive onboarding
document that begins: "We have three problems. They are all related. Don't fix
any of them without understanding all three."

You join as a **Senior Backend Engineer** on the Catalog & Search team. The three
problems are: search quality, the N+1 query disaster in the product catalog API,
and cache invalidation hell. Your first week is all reading. Nnamdi insists.
