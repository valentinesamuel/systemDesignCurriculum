# Leaving PulseCommerce

A year and three months. It's the longest you've stayed at any company since NovaPay.

The work lands. Search relevance improved — merchant churn from "bad search" dropped
from 4.2% to 1.1% in the six months after the Elasticsearch launch. The N+1 query
fix brought the catalog API from 1.8 seconds to 94ms P95. The cache invalidation
architecture ended the stale-price incidents — the sportzone issue was the last one.

The flash sale inventory system is the one you're most proud of. After the Nike
AirMax disaster, PulseCommerce rolled out the reservation queue system. The next
major flash sale — a streetwear brand limited drop, 500 units, 80,000 checkout
attempts in the first 3 minutes — handled it cleanly. Zero oversells. Queue
position visible in real time. Sold out in 7 minutes. The merchant called Nnamdi
personally to say it was the best flash sale experience they'd ever had on any platform.

You leave when Nnamdi announces a strategic pivot to "AI-powered merchandising."
The engineering roadmap becomes unclear. The problems you loved (infrastructure,
consistency, scale) are deprioritized for ML features. You have no regrets about
the year — but the next chapter feels like it should be somewhere with a different
kind of challenge.
