# CivicOS — Company Introduction

CivicOS builds digital government infrastructure — the technology layer that
powers online public services for state and local governments across the United
States and Canada. Their platform processes DMV license renewals, permit applications,
public benefits enrollment, business registration, and voting registration for
23 state clients and dozens of municipalities. 4 million citizen transactions per month.

The mission sounds civic-minded. The engineering reality is unforgiving: government
clients have compliance requirements (FedRAMP, StateRAMP, NIST 800-53) that make
HIPAA look casual. Data sovereignty is explicit and contractual. Rate limiting
isn't just a performance concern — it's a security control. And the citizens using
these systems range from tech-savvy urban professionals to elderly rural residents
with slow connections and 2015-era smartphones.

The engineering team is 35 people. **Jasmyn Crawford**, VP of Engineering, came from
18F (the federal digital services agency) and has both the bureaucratic patience
of a government contractor and the technical rigor of a seasoned system architect.

You join as a **Senior Backend Engineer** on the Security and Platform team. Your
first task is an urgent security review of the rate limiting architecture that Jasmyn
described on the phone: "technically wrong and possibly allowing data enumeration."

Your first meeting is Monday morning. You have the weekend to read the security
disclosure document she sends you Friday night.
