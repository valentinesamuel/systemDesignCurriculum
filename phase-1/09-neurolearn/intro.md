# NeuroLearn — Company Introduction

NeuroLearn builds adaptive learning infrastructure for higher education. Their platform
serves 4.2 million students at 380 universities across North America, the UK, and
Australia — powering adaptive quizzes, study reminders, AI-powered content recommendations,
peer collaboration, and academic integrity monitoring.

The traffic patterns are unlike anything you've seen before. In a typical week, the
platform runs at 15% of peak capacity. During finals week at a large university,
it jumps to 400% of normal load as thousands of students simultaneously access
practice exams, study materials, and receive study reminder notifications. The company
has 380 such "finals week events" per year across different universities in different
time zones — and they overlap.

The engineering team is 50 people. **Obi Mensah**, VP of Engineering, is calm under
pressure in a way that tells you he's been through more crises than he'd like to
admit. He has a phrase he uses often: "The students don't care how it works. They
just need it to work when they need it most." Usually, that's 2 AM before an exam.

You join as a **Senior Backend Engineer** on the Platform team. The first week is all
about reading. Three problems. Three different timelines. All urgent.
