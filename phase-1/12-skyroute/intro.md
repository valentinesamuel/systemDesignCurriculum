# SkyRoute — Company Introduction

SkyRoute is the invisible infrastructure behind global air travel. 47 airline
partners use SkyRoute's platform for flight scheduling, seat inventory management,
passenger check-in, and real-time flight status communications. 380 million
passengers per year interact with SkyRoute-powered systems — they just don't
know it. When a passenger books a seat on Delta through Expedia, receives a
gate change notification at 6:30 AM, or scans their boarding pass at the gate,
they're touching SkyRoute.

The scale is deceptive. The raw annual numbers sound large but the real pressure
is temporal: 24,000 flights per day, and 50% of all passenger interactions
happen in the 90 minutes before departure. That's gate changes, delays, boarding
calls, check-in confirmations, and seat upgrades — for 380 million passengers
per year — compressed into thousands of simultaneous 90-minute windows.

**VP of Engineering Amina Diallo** has led the platform for 6 years. She is
precise, direct, and has one non-negotiable: the 90-minute departure window
cannot fail. "Our airline partners have SLAs with their regulators," she says.
"A gate change notification that arrives after boarding closes is not a late
notification. It's a missed flight."

You join as **Senior Backend Engineer** on the Passenger Experience team —
the team responsible for flight status notifications, search, check-in, and
the customer-facing APIs used by airline partner websites.

"We send 8 million notifications per day," Amina tells you on day one.
"During irregular operations — a summer storm over Chicago, for example —
we send 8 million in 40 minutes. That's when the system breaks. And during
a storm is when passengers need it most."
