# Stratum Systems — Company Introduction

Stratum Systems is a freight forwarding technology company headquartered in Hamburg, Germany, operating at the intersection of global trade and distributed software. They build the data platform that 240-country shipping partners use to track container movements, file customs declarations, and coordinate cross-border logistics for 18 million shipment events per day. It's infrastructure for the physical internet.

You join Stratum as a **Senior Systems Architect** three days after passing the Checkpoint 2 interview. What you didn't know during that interview — the panel member who kept asking pointed questions about clock skew and distributed event ordering — was a Principal Architect at Stratum named **Priya Nair**. She recommended you before you even got the formal offer.

Your first morning, Priya meets you at the Hamburg office coffee machine. She hands you a printout of the clock skew diagram you drew during the interview. She's circled three things in red ink. "These three decisions are why we hired you," she says. "They're also the three things we got wrong two years ago. You have a head start. Don't waste it."

By 10 AM you have a PagerDuty account, access to six Slack channels, and a runbook that was last updated in 2021. By 11 AM, the first alert fires.

Welcome to Stratum Systems.
