# TradeSpark — Transition

Twelve months at TradeSpark. The equity award vested in month eleven. Yuki Tanaka sent a settlement accuracy report to the board: zero phantom positions in the last 6 months. Diana Voss passed the SEC examination with zero material findings. Rachel Kim's mTLS implementation became the template for the firm's security program.

The RocksDB compaction fix — the one that stopped the Monday morning write stalls — got written up in TradeSpark's internal engineering blog. Alex Hartmann forwarded it to three other CTOs. One of them sent a recruiting email.

Yuki's farewell comment: "I've been in risk management for 12 years. The engineers who understand that correctness and performance are the same problem — not competing priorities — are rare. You're one of them."

On your last day, Alex hands you a handwritten note. It's Marcus Webb's updated phone number. "He told me to give this to you when you left. Said you'd know why." You do.

The next week, a LinkedIn message from Amara Chen — the DBA you worked with at Stratum Systems, who has since become CTO of a gig economy platform called GigGrid. She's blunt: "I'm in trouble. My infrastructure is held together with duct tape and optimism. 8M workers, 30 countries, real-time job dispatch, and the job dispatch SLA dashboard has been entirely red for two weeks. I need a Principal Engineer. Are you available?"

You look at your vested equity. You look at Amara's message.

You reply: "When do I start?"
