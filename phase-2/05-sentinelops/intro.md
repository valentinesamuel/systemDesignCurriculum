# SentinelOps — Company Introduction

SentinelOps is a cybersecurity unicorn competing with CrowdStrike and Palo Alto Networks. The platform ingests 40 billion security events per day from endpoint detection tools, network detection systems, and SIEM platforms across 8,000 enterprise customers. The platform identifies threats, correlates alerts, and surfaces the signals that matter from an ocean of noise.

You join as **Lead Systems Architect** following the Apex Financial checkpoint, recruited by CTO **Dr. Aisha Kamara** — a former NSA systems architect who left government to build in the private sector. She reached out the morning after your Apex interview results circulated. Apparently the distributed consensus design impressed someone who knows someone.

"We need someone who can see the whole system," she wrote. "Not just the code. The threat model, the compliance posture, the operational reliability. We're a security company. Our architecture has to be a security argument, not just a technical one."

The office is in London's Canary Wharf. The engineering team is 140 engineers split across 4 pods. Security analysts, product teams, and a 24/7 threat operations center on the same floor.

Day one. You receive the architecture diagram — a single page PDF of 47 microservices connected by arrows that overlap so many times they look like a circuit board.

You notice immediately: no mTLS indicators. No network segmentation annotations. No trust boundaries.

On your second week, a security researcher at a customer company sends a responsible disclosure report that changes everything.
