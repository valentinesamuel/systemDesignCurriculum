# LightspeedRetail — Company Introduction

**Industry**: Enterprise Retail Technology
**Your Role**: Staff Engineer (first Staff-level title)
**Company Style**: "Shopify for enterprise brick-and-mortar"

---

LightspeedRetail is the operating system for enterprise physical retail. 120,000 retail locations across 45 countries run their entire point-of-sale, inventory, and customer experience on LightspeedRetail's platform. Clients range from independent boutique chains to luxury department stores with 400 locations each. The platform processes $2.8 trillion in GMV annually and never — ever — sleeps, because somewhere in the world it is always peak trading hours.

CTO Beatriz Santos recruited you directly. She read the SentinelOps forensics pipeline case study you published internally during your last engagement and sent a cold LinkedIn message at 11PM on a Tuesday: "I read your forensics pipeline writeup. We have a harder problem. When can you talk?" Three interviews later, you have your first Staff Engineer title and a salary that makes the previous three years feel like tuition.

Your first week on the job, Beatriz pulls you aside in the hallway outside your desk orientation. She does not say welcome. She says: "Black Friday is in 47 days. I need you to own the payments and caching architecture before then. The last two Black Fridays we had incidents. The year before that, we had a lawsuit." She hands you a printed incident report and walks away.

You sit down, open your laptop, and read the first line: *"13,400 UK terminals went offline for 22 minutes. 12% of transactions that reconnected resulted in duplicate charges."*

The coffee in your hand is suddenly very inadequate.
