# LightspeedRetail — Company Transition

Black Friday came. Black Friday went.

The numbers came in at 03:47 AM on Saturday: 120,000 terminals online, zero incidents during peak. $4.2 billion processed in 36 hours. A new company record. Beatriz Santos sent a single Slack message to the engineering channel at 04:01 AM: "That's how it's supposed to work."

Three weeks later, she calls you into her office. She offers you the Principal Architect title — a promotion she frames as overdue. "You came in as Staff and solved Staff+1 problems for four months. The title is catching up with the work." The comp package is generous. The scope would be significant.

You thank her. You tell her you need 48 hours to think about it.

That evening, a LinkedIn message arrives from Kofi Mensah. You recognize the name — Kofi was a data engineer at NexaCare during your time there, sharp and quiet, someone who always asked the right question in architecture reviews. He's now VP Engineering at Crestline Energy, a tech subsidiary of a major European utility. "I've been following what you've done with LightspeedRetail," the message reads. "We have a harder problem. Energy data at continental scale. 50 million smart meters across Germany, France, and the UK. We're about to fail a regulatory audit. I think you're the person who can fix it. Principal Systems Architect. When can you talk?"

You reply within the hour.

You tell Beatriz the next morning. She takes it well — better than you expected. "You built something real here," she says. "Go break something new."

You start at Crestline in two weeks.
