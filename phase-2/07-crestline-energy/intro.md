# Crestline Energy — Company Introduction

**Industry**: Smart Grid / Energy Management
**Your Role**: Principal Architect
**Arc**: Chapters 106–112

---

Crestline Energy is not a startup. It is critical infrastructure dressed in software clothes.

Founded in 2009 as a joint venture between three European utility conglomerates, Crestline manages the smart meter data platform for 50 million meters across 12 countries — spanning the UK, Germany, France, Spain, Netherlands, Belgium, and five North American jurisdictions. Every 15 minutes, every one of those meters phones home. That is 200 million events per hour, 4.8 billion rows per day, arriving in parallel streams from four different meter vendor protocols, through six regional data centers, into a platform that was originally designed for 3 million meters in 2011.

The platform did not grow gracefully. It grew the way old cities grow — new roads built on top of old roads, new pipes running alongside old pipes, nobody quite sure what the original map looked like anymore.

NERC CIP compliance is mandatory — not aspirational, not "we're working toward it," but mandatory under regulation with multi-million-euro fines for non-compliance. ISO 27001 certification must be renewed annually. In France, energy consumption data for citizens is classified as strategic national infrastructure. In the UK, Ofgem can open a formal investigation and demand a complete grid state snapshot going back 90 days.

You join as Principal Architect. The recruiter specifically mentioned that Beatriz Santos, your former CTO at LightspeedRetail, gave you "the strongest reference she had ever written." That got the door open. What keeps it open is day one.

Elena Vasquez, CTO, meets you at your desk on your first morning. She does not say welcome. She sits down across from you, sets a coffee on your desk, and asks: "The data pipeline runs an 18-hour batch job nightly. Why should I care?"

You are expected to know what is wrong. You are expected to fix it.

The clock starts now.
