# Crestline Energy — Transition

Eleven months at Crestline Energy.

The 18-hour batch job is gone. The Flink streaming pipeline produces demand forecasts continuously, with p99 freshness under 8 minutes. The Ofgem investigation into the March 3rd incident closed with a finding of "technical compliance" — Crestline provided the requested data within 14 days, not 30. Elena Vasquez quietly sends you an email: *"14 days was not the minimum. It was the right answer. Thank you."*

The France contract signed on day 58, two days before the deadline. Lena Krause describes the contract annexe as "the clearest technical specification I have ever attached to a legal document." That's the highest praise she gives anyone.

The bi-temporal data model is in production across all 12 countries. Three months in, a German regulator submits a data request that would previously have taken 3 days to answer. You answer it in 11 minutes. Elena Vasquez watches over your shoulder and says nothing, which means everything.

On your last day, Elena offers you a VP of Engineering track. The equity is real. The work has been meaningful.

You turn it down because VenueFlow has been persistent.

Their infrastructure challenge is completely different from grid stability: unpredictable demand spikes. 50 million tickets sold in 48 hours when Taylor Swift announces a new tour. 2 million users, 80,000 tickets, 90 seconds. Sustained load vs spike load — an entirely different set of problems.

You've been doing sustained load for two years. You want to solve something that breaks differently.
