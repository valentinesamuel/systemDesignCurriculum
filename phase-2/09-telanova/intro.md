# TeleNova — Company Introduction

## Carrier-Grade Infrastructure at 120 Million Subscribers

TeleNova is the third-largest mobile carrier in Europe by subscriber count: 120 million subscribers across 8 countries, with a network spanning the Netherlands, Belgium, Austria, Denmark, Finland, and three others. It is not a startup. It is not a scale-up. It is a telecommunications company that was founded in 1991, has run the same billing system since 2007, and is attempting to launch 5G in 90 days.

You join as Principal Architect through a connection you didn't expect — Lars Engström, TeleNova's Head of Network Engineering, saw the VenueFlow engineering blog post about the Taylor Swift onsale architecture. He sent you a LinkedIn message. Three calls later, you are flying to Amsterdam.

The CTO is Dr. Ingrid Hoffman. Ericsson veteran, 22 years. She has an engineering PhD from Delft. She speaks in precise sentences and does not waste words. Your first meeting with her lasts exactly forty-five minutes. She slides a laminated runbook across the table within the first five minutes — it is dated 2019 and covers incident response for the 4G core network. Then she opens a laptop and shows you a Slack message from the 5G program director: "5G launch is in 90 days."

"We have," she says, "significant technical debt at the infrastructure layer that predates my tenure by several years. Some of it is acceptable. Some of it is not acceptable when 120 million subscribers are depending on it." She closes the laptop. "I need someone who can look at our service architecture, our observability stack, our data pipelines, and tell me what will break first under 5G load — and how to fix it before it breaks."

She does not ask you if you can do it. She assumes you can. That, you realize, is the highest form of confidence a technical leader can show.

The 5G launch is in 90 days. You start Monday.
