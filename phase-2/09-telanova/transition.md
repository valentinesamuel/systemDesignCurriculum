# TeleNova — Transition

Eleven months.

The 6-hour Kafka consumer lag is now a 4-second lag. The Istio latency overhead dropped from 12ms to 3.2ms per hop — SIM activation p99 has been under 180ms for 8 consecutive months. The 5-country expansion launched on schedule. Poland and Czech Republic were smooth. Romania had a 4-hour delay because the regulator's approval portal was offline on launch morning. You and Fatima had a contingency plan ready. Croatia went live 3 days early because the construction team finished the network infrastructure ahead of schedule.

At the month 10 all-hands, Alejandro Reyes says: "We added 8 million subscribers and the engineering team ran smoother than when we added 2 million. That's what good architecture looks like."

Fatima Al-Rashid says goodbye in the corridor, not with sentimentality but with precision: "You solved the problems that were on your list and two that weren't. Come back when you need a reference."

The call from Thomas Adeyemi comes through LinkedIn. BuildRight — construction technology. 40,000 active construction projects, 80 countries, 183 days until a compliance audit. The previous Staff Architect left 8 months ago. The architecture documentation is 5 years out of date.

You've done telecommunications and energy grid and cybersecurity. You've never done construction.

You accept because the compliance audit has a hard wall and hard walls require clear thinking, not industry expertise. You pack your laptop and take the train to BuildRight's London office.

There's a sticky note on the monitor at your new desk: *"Compliance audit in 6 months. Start with the architecture docs. — T.A."*

The countdown clock in the legal department reads 183 days.

You start reading.
