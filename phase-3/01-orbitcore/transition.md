# Transition: OrbitCore → IronWatch

Eight months at OrbitCore felt like eight years compressed — satellite telemetry pipelines, cell-based fault isolation three hundred miles above the Earth, a capacity planning exercise that accounted for solar activity as an input variable. You shipped. You broke things at constellation scale and fixed them faster than anyone expected. On your last day, your Staff Engineer title was formally ratified in a brief all-hands where Director of Engineering Yara Osman said, simply, "They made us better." Marcus Webb's farewell DM from two weeks prior still sat at the top of your Slack — you hadn't archived it, and you weren't sure you would. Your next engagement is nothing like orbit: IronWatch, a defense contractor building a threat intelligence platform for government analysts, reached out through a consulting broker. The work is classified. The onboarding involves an NDA, a background check, and something called a SCIF. You've built systems for hospitals, airlines, and satellites — but you've never built anything you couldn't tell your friends about, and that particular unease follows you all the way to the IronWatch parking lot on Monday morning, badge lanyard in hand.
