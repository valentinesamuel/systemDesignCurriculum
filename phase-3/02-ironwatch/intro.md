# IronWatch — Company Introduction

IronWatch is a defense contractor headquartered in Reston, Virginia, building a multi-classification threat intelligence platform used by analysts across seventeen government agencies. You arrive not as a new hire but as an external consultant, which means your first day involves three forms of ID, an NDA with a page count you don't bother to count, a badge that grants access to exactly two rooms, and an escort to a SCIF — Sensitive Compartmented Information Facility — where phones are stored in a numbered locker outside the door and the air feels slightly different, quieter, more deliberate. The CISO, Colonel Marguerite Osei (retired Army signals intelligence, now civilian), meets you inside and says: "You're here because our previous architect left us with a system that works until it doesn't. We need it to not not-work." The platform serves 500 analysts across three clearance tiers. Your job is to make sure data flows in exactly one direction, access is auditable down to the keystroke, and the architecture survives an Inspector General review in ninety days.
