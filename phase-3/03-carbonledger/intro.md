# CarbonLedger — Company Introduction

## The Company

CarbonLedger is a climate tech startup building the world's most trusted carbon credit registry. Founded in 2021 by a former UNFCCC negotiator and a distributed systems engineer who left Google to "work on something that actually matters," CarbonLedger sits at the intersection of environmental regulation and enterprise software. The company's mission is deceptively simple: make every carbon credit traceable, verifiable, and permanent — forever.

The voluntary carbon market has been plagued by scandals. Double-counted credits. Phantom projects. Registries that lost records. CarbonLedger was built as a direct response to that chaos, with an immutable ledger architecture at its core. The company currently manages 50 million carbon credits across 600 project developers and 1,400 corporate buyers in 47 countries.

## The Industry

Carbon markets are entering their most consequential decade. The Paris Agreement's Article 6 provisions — finally operationalized at COP28 — created a framework for countries to trade carbon credits internationally. The prize: a single international registry trusted by 196 nations. The risk: if that registry is wrong, the climate math is wrong.

CarbonLedger is in the running for the UN contract. This is not a product launch. This is an infrastructure procurement that will determine whether the international carbon market is trustworthy or not.

## Your Role

You're joining as a Senior Backend Engineer three weeks before COP31 in Nairobi. The engineering team is 14 people. The VP of Engineering, Yara Osei, interviewed you herself and made the offer in 48 hours. You accepted in 24. You've been told the team is "good but spread thin." What you discover on day one is that the architecture was designed for the voluntary market and was never meant to carry the weight of international sovereign compliance.
