# Transition: CarbonLedger → LexCore

The UN Article 6 adoption spike broke three things simultaneously and you helped fix all three without sleeping for forty hours — that was CarbonLedger's goodbye gift to you. Six months later the platform is stable, the new engineering team runs standby rotations without paging you, and the VP of Engineering sends a farewell note that says, in part, "You built the thing that made this company real." You sit with that for a moment in the airport departures hall, laptop bag over one shoulder, then close the tab and open a new one. LexCore, a legal AI company serving AmLaw 100 firms, found you through the same consulting broker who placed you at IronWatch. The pitch was simple: they're building AI-assisted legal research and document review, and the access control story is a disaster waiting to happen. It already happened once — a paralegal saw the wrong client's documents on day two of a pilot — and the CEO wants a Staff-level engineer embedded before the next firm goes live. Law is a world you've never touched: privileged communications, conflict-of-interest rules, courts of law. You remind yourself that access control is access control, data is data — then you board the flight and wonder, not for the first time, whether that's actually true.
