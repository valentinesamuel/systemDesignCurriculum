# LexCore — Company Introduction

LexCore is a legal AI startup headquartered in New York City, building AI-assisted research and document review tools for AmLaw 100 law firms — the hundred largest firms in the United States. Their platform indexes tens of millions of legal documents, delivers AI-generated research summaries, and recently launched a "matter workspace" feature that lets attorneys collaborate on active client matters within the platform. You join as a new hire, embedded Staff Engineer, brought in specifically because the access control story is not ready for the customer growth the sales team has already booked. Your first morning, before you've finished onboarding paperwork, the VP of Engineering Sasha Petrenko drops a printed incident report on your desk: three days ago, during a pilot onboarding for Hartwell & Morse LLP, a paralegal named Cameron Ruiz accidentally accessed a document from a different client matter — not their client, not their matter, not their firm. The document was a due diligence memo for an active M&A transaction. The firm's managing partner called LexCore's CEO directly. You are the response.
