# AutoMesh — Arc Introduction

**Industry**: Autonomous Vehicles / Connected Vehicle Platform
**Your Role**: External Consultant (Staff-Level Architecture Review)
**Arc Chapters**: Ch. 193 – Ch. 200

---

AutoMesh is a connected vehicle platform startup that has spent four years building software for commercial autonomous freight fleets — think long-haul trucks, port logistics vehicles, and last-mile delivery robots. They are technically ambitious and safety-obsessed, but they have never operated at consumer scale, and their architecture reflects that: purpose-built for predictable commercial environments with controlled schedules and known vehicle behaviors.

Three weeks ago, AutoMesh won a contract with the City of San Francisco and the California Department of Transportation to deploy a 50-mile smart transportation corridor covering the I-280 corridor into downtown — 200 instrumented intersections, adaptive traffic signals, and support for up to 10,000 connected vehicles from multiple manufacturers. The contract is worth $180M over five years, and it is the largest V2X (Vehicle-to-Everything) deployment in North America.

You are brought in as an external consultant — not a permanent hire — because AutoMesh's CTO, Dr. Priscilla Nakamura, wants a Staff-level architecture review before they countersign the deployment agreement. The city's technical liaison has already raised questions about their FMEA documentation and NHTSA certification posture, and someone in the building needs to give an honest answer before ink hits paper.

Your engagement is time-boxed: eight weeks, clear deliverables, no guarantee of extension. You have seen this kind of architecture before — built by brilliant engineers who optimized for the happy path. Your job is to find every place the system fails, and make sure each one has an answer.
