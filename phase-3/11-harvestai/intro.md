# HarvestAI — Company Introduction

HarvestAI sits at the intersection of climate urgency and agricultural pragmatism: a precision agriculture platform managing 50 million acres across North America and Brazil, threading together soil sensors, drone imagery, satellite feeds, and edge ML inference into a single coherent intelligence layer for 8,000 farm operators who mostly do not care about Kubernetes but very much care about whether their soybeans survive a dry April. The company is scrappy in the way that only a 12-person engineering team stretched across two continents can be — everyone carries more context than their job title suggests, the on-call rotation is short, and the architecture decisions from two years ago are visible in the production database like geological strata. You join as a new hire Staff Engineer in late January, which means your honeymoon period will last exactly sixty days before planting season arrives and the system faces its annual trial by fire.

Your first morning, Priya Sundaram, Head of Engineering, hands you a laptop and a coffee and says, without preamble: "The geospatial queries are a disaster. The carbon credit integration is half-built. We have sixty days." There is something grounding about working in agricultural technology — the domain has a physical weight that most software does not. The problems are abstract in execution but concrete in consequence: a delayed drought alert means a farmer does not irrigate in time, and a season's worth of work dies in a field. The metaphorical dirt here is real somewhere, and you feel it on your first day, sitting in a San Francisco office, reading query plans that are trying and failing to describe fifty million acres of living earth.
