# PropIQ — Company Introduction

**Industry**: Real Estate Technology / Commercial Property Intelligence
**Your Role**: New Hire — Staff Engineer
**Arc**: Chapters 238–243

---

PropIQ sells intelligence to the commercial real estate industry. Not listings — intelligence. There's a difference, and it matters to everyone in the company. Listings are what Zillow does. PropIQ tells you that a 47-story office tower in Midtown Manhattan is 23% vacant, was built in 1989, had its HVAC system replaced in 2021, is owned through a CMBS trust with a balloon payment due in 18 months, sits in a flood zone that's been reclassified twice since 2010, and had three environmental remediation notices filed with the EPA in the last decade. That is intelligence. Investment decisions — $50M, $100M, $200M — are made using PropIQ data every week.

The clients are commercial real estate brokers, institutional investors, and private equity firms. The data comes from 12 sources: county assessors (quarterly updates), Multiple Listing Service feeds (daily), building permit databases (weekly), satellite imagery (monthly), environmental report aggregators, FEMA flood maps, CMBS loan records, CoStar licensing feeds, and four others. Each source has different data quality, different update frequencies, different licensing terms, and different levels of accuracy. PropIQ's job is to reconcile them into a coherent, trustworthy picture of 5 million commercial properties across the United States.

You join on a Tuesday. By Thursday afternoon, the VP of Product has pinged you on Slack: "Hey — just FYI — the pricing analytics dashboard is taking 45 seconds to load for some of our bigger clients. Sales is getting complaints. No pressure, but the enterprise contract renewal for Meridian Capital is in 6 weeks and they're the ones complaining loudest." That's your welcome to PropIQ. The data is slow, the clients are impatient, and the enterprise contract is 6 weeks out.
