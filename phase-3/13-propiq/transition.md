You spent six months at PropIQ and left a data platform that actually deserved the word "platform." On your last Friday, Marcus Tran found you in the kitchen at 5:45 PM, nursing cold coffee. He didn't do small talk. He said: "That data mesh redesign you pushed through in your first month — I fought it for two years before you got here. Two years. You walked in, drew four boxes on a whiteboard, and Divya approved it in a week." He shook his head, not bitterly, but with the exhaustion of someone who had finally been heard after a long silence. "And the OCR cron job. I've been ashamed of that cron job for three years. It ran off my laptop. My personal laptop. Every morning." He laughed once, short and flat. "You didn't even make me feel stupid about it. You just said 'this is a reliability problem, not a character flaw.' I've been telling junior engineers that same line ever since." Divya Nair walked you out at 6 PM, handshake firm, expression unreadable in the way of someone who keeps their respect closely held. At the door she said: "I hope you know what you're walking into next." You said you didn't. She nodded like that was the right answer. You stepped out into the parking lot with your laptop bag over one shoulder and the quiet certainty that you'd left something better than you found it — which, you were beginning to understand, was the only metric that mattered.
