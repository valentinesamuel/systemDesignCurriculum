# Arc 14: NovaSports — Sports Tech / Real-Time Fan Engagement

## Company Overview

NovaSports powers the official digital fan experience for the National Athletic League (NAL) — a major professional sports league with 30 teams, 82-game regular seasons, and a championship watched by 60 million people. The platform delivers live game data, personalized feeds, in-game statistics, and a sportsbook integration to 35 million registered app users across the US and a rapidly growing European market.

The company sits at the intersection of real-time data engineering, machine learning personalization, and high-stakes reliability — if the app goes down during a championship final, it is national news within minutes. The engineering team is 120 people, split between platform, product, and data science. Infrastructure spend is $1.8M/month and climbing.

## Your Role

You join as a Staff Engineer embedded in the Platform team, reporting to Priya Nair — yes, the same Priya Nair who hired you at PrismHealth, who has since moved to NovaSports as VP of Engineering. She called you personally. "I watched you redesign that patient monitoring pipeline under pressure. I need that energy here. The championship is in 8 months."

## First Day

Your first day is a Monday in early October, the start of the regular season. By 9 AM, there is already a Slack alert in #platform-on-call: a spike in fan feed latency during the opening game's tip-off. Nobody pages an incident — the spike resolves in 40 seconds. By 10 AM, you have read the postmortem from last year's championship outage. By noon, the CEO, Dominic Eze, stops by your desk. He does not introduce himself. He just says: "The app went down in Q4. My phone did not stop ringing for a week. Fix it." Then he walks away.

You open your laptop and start reading the architecture docs. They are two years out of date.
