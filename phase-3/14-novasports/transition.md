# Leaving NovaSports

The chaos engineering incident that became a real incident taught you more about blast radius and system coupling than any design review ever could. You spent the final weeks at NovaSports doing something you hadn't done before: writing the architecture review document not just as a technical artifact, but as a story — a narrative of how a system grew beyond its own documentation, how undiscovered dependencies accumulate in the dark corners of a fast-moving codebase, and how the most dangerous failure is not the one you planned for but the one you assumed couldn't happen. You shared it with the whole engineering org. Dev thanked you privately. Marcus Webb, who saw the document somehow through channels you never traced, sent a single Slack message at 11:47 PM on a Friday: "Good postmortem. The parking lot section was the bravest part." You didn't reply that night. Some compliments need to sit.

Your next role is at MindScale, a behavioral analytics company that helps Fortune 500 employers run employee wellness programs. On your first day, the privacy officer hands you a civil liberties attorney's report before you've had a chance to find the bathroom.
