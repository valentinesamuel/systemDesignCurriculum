# MindScale — Company Introduction

**Industry:** Mental Health Tech / Behavioral Analytics for Enterprise Wellness
**Your Role:** New Hire — Staff Engineer (joining a team of 22 engineers)
**Company Size:** Series B, 180 employees, $47M raised

---

MindScale is not the kind of company you can explain at a dinner party without everyone either leaning forward or leaning back. The product: behavioral analytics software deployed inside Fortune 500 companies to help HR departments run employee wellness programs. In practice, MindScale's platform ingests passive signals — app usage patterns, communication metadata, calendar density, self-reported mood check-ins, and optional biometric data from wearable integrations — and surfaces insights to people managers and wellness coordinators. The pitch to enterprise customers is compelling: early identification of burnout, stress cascades, and mental health risk before they become medical leave events. The business metrics are real — customers report 18% reduction in unplanned leave and 12% reduction in benefits costs.

The ethical complexity is also real.

Your role is Staff Engineer, responsible for platform infrastructure. The engineering team is smart and genuinely well-intentioned — they believe in what they're building. The CTO, Dr. Priya Okonkwo (different person from NovaSports, same name is a coincidence the universe enjoys), came from Stanford's human-computer interaction lab. She cares deeply about privacy. The problem is that the business grew faster than its privacy architecture. The enterprise contracts are real. The data flows are real. And on your first day, before you have a badge photo taken, the privacy officer walks over, sits down across from you at the on-boarding desk, and puts a printed document in front of you.

"I've been waiting for a staff engineer with infrastructure depth," she says. "This is a civil liberties attorney's report arguing that our platform violates employees' privacy rights. I need you to understand if they're technically correct."

You pick up the report. You start reading.
