# GlacierGrid — Company Introduction

**Industry**: Distributed Renewable Energy / Virtual Power Plant Operations
**Headquarters**: San Francisco, CA (with operations in Austin, TX and Munich, Germany)
**Your Role**: Staff Engineer, Grid Systems — new hire

---

GlacierGrid is a virtual power plant (VPP) operator — which means it doesn't own a single power plant, and yet it controls more dispatchable energy capacity than most utilities. It does this by aggregating 500,000 distributed energy resources (DERs) across three markets: California (CAISO), Texas (ERCOT), and Germany (the German Bundesnetz). Those resources are homeowner solar panels paired with Tesla Powerwalls, EV chargers enrolled in smart-charging programs, commercial HVAC systems in office buildings, and community battery installations the size of shipping containers. Each device is an endpoint. Collectively, they are a power plant.

The company makes money three ways: selling demand response capacity to grid operators (someone pays GlacierGrid to reduce load when the grid is stressed), participating in frequency regulation markets (earning fees for keeping grid frequency stable), and optimization services sold to utilities (helping them defer expensive transmission upgrades). The engineering team is 80 people. The grid operations team is 12 people who watch screens 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year.
